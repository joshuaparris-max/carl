Class & stats system

The dungeon's class selection is book-critical

Carl's path

  • Starts classless — the system offers a selection box
  • Must choose before entering Floor 1
  • Book-accurate classes: Crawler, Barbarian, Rogue, Healer, Enchanter, Monk
  • Carl eventually leans physical/brawler due to no formal magic
  • No Pants debuff is a real mechanical penalty to armor

Donut's path

  • Starts Level 0, civilian, no class
  • First kills unlock her class selection separately
  • Her trajectory: toward magic/support (book-accurate)
  • Cat traits: agility bonus, low carry weight, claw attacks
  • Grand Champion title grants cosmetic fame stat

The Syndicate & viewer system

The show-within-a-game meta layer

Viewer sponsorships

  • Viewers are alien races watching the dungeon as entertainment
  • Sponsorships drop items into the dungeon in real time
  • Popularity stat affects quality and frequency of drops
  • Donut is more naturally popular — players must manage this
  • Controversial actions spike viewership (book-accurate)

The Syndicate faction

  • NPC crawlers who are organized criminals using the dungeon
  • Can ally, betray, or ignore — all have consequences
  • Book-accurate: they run the saferoom economy
  • Princess Donut's social skills unlock Syndicate dialogue trees
  • Carl's bluntness closes dialogue options Donut opens

Lore accuracy requirements

Things the game must get right that most adaptations miss

Carl's jacket is not optional flavor. The leather jacket is explicitly described as the one piece of real protection he has. It must be a named, stats-bearing item in the inventory system — not a cosmetic. Its durability matters to the plot of book 1.
Donut's breed and title are mechanically significant. She is a Grand Champion Persian show cat. Her high Charisma/social stats should trace directly to this. She is not just Carl's pet — she is a co-protagonist with a separate stat sheet and separate class evolution.
The System is a character. Announcements must be voiced as unhinged, sarcastic, and darkly comedic. The System mocks crawlers, hypes deaths, and editorialize outcomes. It is not a neutral HUD — it has a personality. All System notifications should feel like they were written by a deranged game-show host.
The dungeon is a literal alien TV show. This must be felt in gameplay — viewer counts, sponsor drops, and popularity ratings are real mechanics. Carl's unpredictability and Donut's charm are their primary survival advantages beyond combat.
Mordecai's stress is a running gag with real stakes. He genuinely cares about Carl and Donut surviving. His Chupacabra form is his manager form — he has other forms. His dialogue should degrade in composure as danger increases.
No pants is a real debuff, not a joke. Carl's lack of pants must impose a specific, named armor penalty. Book 1 hinges on this detail — the system catalogues him exactly as found. His later gear upgrades reference this origin.
Explosions are Carl's primary language. Crafting explosive items from dungeon materials is not a side system — it is Carl's core combat identity. He is not a magic user. He is a man who figures out how to blow things up with whatever is available.
The floors have collapse timers. Each floor has a hard deadline before it is destroyed and any remaining crawlers with it. This is book-accurate and must create real time pressure in gameplay — not just a background element.

The System interface

Pop-up notifications in dungeon-OS style. All text must be sarcastic, dark-comedic, and specific. Examples: "Achievement unlocked: Still Alive. Reward: Nothing yet." — "Congratulations, Crawler #4,411! You have failed to die in the first thirty seconds. The viewers are disappointed." System notifications gate class selection, announce floor timers, confirm kills, and editorialize combat outcomes.

Critical

Dual protagonist system

Carl and Donut have completely separate stat sheets, separate class trees, and separate social reputations. Donut's Charisma is off the charts. Carl's is average. Donut opens dialogue doors Carl cannot. Carl opens combat solutions Donut cannot. The game must require both to be used — not just Carl with Donut as a pet.

High priority

Crafting — improvised explosives

Carl's primary combat style. The crafting system must support: combining dungeon materials into explosive devices, discovering recipes through experimentation (not just menus), variable yield based on materials used, and the ability to improvise during combat. This is not a side system — it is Carl's character identity.

High priority

Viewer popularity & sponsorships

A live popularity meter visible to the player. Specific actions spike it: creative kills, funny moments, unexpected survival. Donut's appearance and Carl's chaotic behavior are the primary drivers. Sponsors send item drops mid-dungeon. Different alien viewer factions have different tastes — pleasing one might displease another.

Important

Floor timer & collapse mechanic

Each floor has a visible countdown. When it hits zero, the floor collapses and kills remaining crawlers. This creates mandatory pacing — players cannot grind indefinitely on a single floor. The timer also affects what NPCs are willing to help with (a Syndicate member won't barter with 2 minutes left).

pacingtensionbook-accurate
Important

Saferooms economy

Saferooms are the only truly safe zones. They have shops, rest mechanics, and NPC interaction. The Syndicate partially controls saferoom commerce. Prices respond to floor danger level. Donut's social stats can haggle; Carl's bluntness gets a flat price. Some saferoom-only items are critical for survival on the next floor.

Book-accurate character specs

What the game must capture about each major character

Carl Estanza

Starting state (book 1, chapter 1)

  • Wearing: boxer shorts, leather jacket, socks — nothing else
  • No class, Level 1 crawler
  • Background: worked odd jobs, owned the apartment Donut lived in
  • Combat style: improvised, explosive, physical brawler
  • Social style: blunt to a fault, accidentally honest, unexpectedly effective
  • Key trait: he refuses to give up even when giving up is the smart play
  • Relationship with Donut: he pretends she's just the cat, she knows she runs things
Princess Donut

Starting state (book 1, chapter 1)

  • Persian cat, show breed, Grand Champion pedigree — this is her whole identity
  • Level 0 civilian — she has zero combat experience
  • Charisma stat is already high at level 0 due to breed and title
  • Complains constantly — but specifically about smell, dirt, and indignity
  • Is immediately more popular with viewers than Carl
  • Her magic aptitude emerges naturally — she did not choose it consciously
  • She genuinely loves Carl but would never say so in those words
Mordecai

The manager — book-accurate portrayal

  • Appears in Chupacabra form in the Tutorial Guild — this is his manager/work form
  • Is visibly stressed from the first line of dialogue
  • Deeply competent but dealing with genuinely impossible clients
  • Has real affection for both Carl and Donut that he expresses through exasperation
  • Knows far more about the dungeon's true nature than he can reveal
  • His dialogue should degrade from professional → frantic → barely holding it together
  • He is not comic relief — he is a tragic character playing a comedy role

World design — floor by floor

Book-accurate floor environments and what makes each one distinct

Floor 1

The savannas — book 1

Open grassland biome. Not underground — it is sky-lit via illusion (the dungeon creates fake environments). Basic enemies: kobolds, goblin scouts, crawling insects the size of dogs. The first gladiator arena entrance appears here. Carl discovers improvised explosives are devastatingly effective. Donut earns her first kill and immediately begins asking about her viewer count.

Floor 2

The city ruins — book 2

A destroyed city environment. More complex navigation — buildings to enter, sewer systems, rooftop routes. The Syndicate is more present here. Carl's reputation from Floor 1 precedes him. More complex enemy factions with internal politics. Donut begins her magic development in earnest. The viewer popularity system becomes more complex — factions of alien viewers start taking sides.

Floor 3

The Desperado Club level — book 3

Carl becomes a named celebrity in the dungeon. The Desperado Club is a massive, book-accurate saferoom/entertainment complex that serves as a major story hub. Social mechanics peak here — Donut is a bona fide star. Major Syndicate plot threads resolve and complicate. New crawler allies (and betrayals) happen here. This floor has the most complex environment of the first three books.

Saferooms (all floors)

Consistent saferoom rules

Every saferoom must have: a shop (Syndicate-adjacent pricing), a rest mechanic, at least one NPC with a unique personality, no enemies, and a door that locks from the inside. Saferoom shops stock items relevant to the next floor — this is a subtle navigation hint the books use. The Syndicate's presence in saferooms escalates as floors deepen.

Recommended tech stack

How to take the Python prototype into a real game

Option A — 2D indie

Pygame / Godot

  • Pygame: extend the prototype directly, add sprites, tile maps, sound
  • Godot 4: free, Python-adjacent (GDScript), excellent for tile-based games
  • Best for solo dev or small team
  • Grid movement maps naturally from the prototype
Option B — CRPG style

Unity (2D)

  • More polish ceiling for isometric or top-down view
  • C# is a step up from Python but well-documented
  • Asset store helps with dungeon tile sets
  • Better for teams with a visual design focus

Data architecture — what needs to be modular

  • All dialogue in JSON — Mordecai, Donut, System announcements. Never hard-code strings into game logic.
  • Stats as data — every character stat, debuff, and modifier in a data file, not hardcoded. The "No Pants" debuff is a record in a debuffs table.
  • Floor maps as tiled data — Tiled map editor exports to JSON; Godot and Pygame both import it natively.
  • System notifications as templates — write a notification engine that fills in crawler name, kill count, item found into pre-written sarcastic templates.
  • Crafting recipes as a graph — items and their combinations stored as nodes, making recipe discovery easy to extend without touching code.
First milestone target: A playable Tutorial Guild + Floor 1 entry corridor, with the System interface showing sarcastic notifications, Donut following Carl with her own stat display, and one working crafting combination that produces an explosion. That is a demo worth showing.
